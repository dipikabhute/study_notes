1. Explain Philosophical Aspects of Intellectual Property Laws

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are based on different philosophical theories which explain why these rights are given to creators.

1. Natural Rights Theory
   This theory states that a person has the right over what they create. If a person invents something, it belongs to them.

Example:
A software developer creates a program → He owns it.

2. Reward Theory
   This theory says creators should get rewards for their work.

Example:
Scientist invents medicine → Gets patent → Earns profit

3. Incentive Theory
   IPR encourages people to create new inventions.

Example:
Developers create new apps to earn money.

4. Economic Growth Theory
   Innovation leads to economic growth.

Example:
New technology improves industries.

5. Social Benefit Theory
   Society benefits from inventions.

Example:
Medical inventions save lives.

2. Explain Basic Principles of Patent Law

Patent is a legal right given to inventor to protect invention.

Patent Requirements:

1. Novelty – Invention must be new
2. Utility – Invention must be useful
3. Non-Obviousness – Invention should not be obvious

Patent Validity: 20 years

Examples:
New machine
New software
New medical device
