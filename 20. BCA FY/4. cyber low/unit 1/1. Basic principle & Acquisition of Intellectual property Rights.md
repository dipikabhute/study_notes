1. Introduction to Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)
Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are legal rights given to creators for their inventions, designs, works, and innovations.

Examples:
Invention → Patent

Book → Copyright

Logo → Trademark

Product Design → Design Rights

Types of Intellectual Property
Patent

Copyright

Trademark

Design Rights

Trade Secrets

Geographical Indication

2. Philosophical Aspects of Intellectual Property Laws
Philosophy behind IPR explains why intellectual property rights exist.

Main Philosophical Theories
1. Natural Rights Theory
Creator has natural right over their creation

Based on effort and creativity

Example: Writer owns their book

2. Reward Theory
Innovation should be rewarded

Encourages inventors to create new things

3. Economic Theory
Encourages economic growth

Promotes innovation and competition

4. Public Benefit Theory
Innovation benefits society

Knowledge shared after protection period ends

3. Basic Principles of Patent Law
A Patent gives exclusive rights to inventor for their invention.

Conditions for Patent (Patentability Criteria)
Novelty

Must be new invention

Inventive Step

Must not be obvious

Industrial Application

Must be useful in industry

Patent Rights
Exclusive rights to make

Sell invention

License invention

Patent Duration
20 years from filing date

Patent Example
Mobile battery saving technology → Patent

4. Patent Application Procedure
Steps for Applying Patent:

Step 1: Idea / Invention
Inventor develops idea

Step 2: Patent Search
Check if invention already exists

Step 3: Draft Patent Application
Prepare patent document

Step 4: Filing Application
Submit application to Patent Office

Step 5: Publication
Application published after 18 months

Step 6: Examination
Patent examiner checks application

Step 7: Grant of Patent
Patent granted if approved

Types of Patent Applications
Provisional Application

Complete Application

Convention Application

PCT Application

5. Drafting of Patent Specification
Patent specification is technical document describing invention

Types of Patent Specification
1. Provisional Specification
Filed when invention not fully completed

2. Complete Specification
Full invention details

Contents of Patent Specification
Title of invention

Field of invention

Background of invention

Summary of invention

Detailed description

Claims

Drawings (if required)

Abstract

6. Understanding Copyright Law
Copyright protects creative works

Examples
Books

Music

Movies

Software

Paintings

Features of Copyright
Automatic protection

No registration required (optional)

Rights Under Copyright
Right to reproduce

Right to distribute

Right to perform

Right to display

Copyright Duration
Life of author + 60 years (India)

Software Copyright Example
Computer Program → Copyright Protection

Based on
World Intellectual Property Organization
and
Copyright Act 1957

7. Basic Principles of Trademark
Trademark protects brand identity

Trademark Examples
Logo

Brand name

Symbol

Tagline

Trademark Characteristics
Unique

Distinctive

Non-deceptive

Trademark Rights
Exclusive use of brand

Legal protection

Trademark Duration
10 years (Renewable forever)

Example:
Nike logo → Trademark

Governed by
Trade Marks Act 1999

8. Basic Principles of Design Rights
Design Rights protect appearance of product

Protects:
Shape

Pattern

Color

Ornament

Examples:
Mobile design

Bottle shape

Car design

Design Rights Duration
10 years + 5 years extension

Governed by
Designs Act 2000

9. International Background of Intellectual Property
IPR is protected internationally through treaties.

Major International Organizations
1. World Intellectual Property Organization
Promotes global IP protection

2. World Trade Organization
Handles global trade and IP laws

Important International Agreements
1. TRIPS Agreement
Global minimum IP standards

2. Paris Convention
Patent & Trademark protection

3. Berne Convention
Copyright protection

Summary (Exam Quick Revision)
Type	Protects	Duration
Patent	Invention	20 Years
Copyright	Creative Work	Life + 60 Years
Trademark	Brand	10 Years (Renewable)
Design	Product Design	10 + 5 Years
