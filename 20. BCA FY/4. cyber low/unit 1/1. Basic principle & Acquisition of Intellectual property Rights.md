## 1. Introduction to Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to creations of human mind such as:

* Inventions
* Literary works
* Music
* Designs
* Symbols
* Names
* Images

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are legal rights given to creators to protect their creations.

Examples:

* New invention → Patent
* Song → Copyright
* Brand name → Trademark
* Product design → Design Right

---

## 2. Philosophical Aspects of Intellectual Property Laws

Philosophical aspects explain why IPR laws exist.

1. Natural Rights Theory
   Creator owns what they create.

2. Reward Theory
   Creators should get reward for their work.

3. Incentive Theory
   Encourages innovation.

4. Economic Growth Theory
   Innovation improves economy.

5. Social Benefit Theory
   Society benefits from inventions.

---

## 3. Basic Principles of Patent Law

Patent is a legal right given to inventor to protect invention.

Patent validity in India: 20 years

Requirements of Patent:

1. Novel (New)
2. Useful
3. Non-obvious

Examples:

* New software
* New machine
* New technology

## 6. Understanding Copyright Law

Copyright protects creative works.

Examples:

* Books
* Music
* Movies
* Software

Validity:

Life of author + 60 years

Rights:

* Copy
* Publish
* Sell
* Distribute

---

## 7. Basic Principles of Trademark

Trademark protects brand name or logo.

Examples:

* Brand name
* Logo
* Symbol

Validity:

10 years (renewable)

---

## 8. Basic Principles of Design Rights

Design right protects product appearance.

Examples:

* Mobile design
* Bottle design
* Car design

Validity:

10 years extendable
