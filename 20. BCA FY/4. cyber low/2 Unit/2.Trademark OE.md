# 1️⃣ Trademark – Meaning

A Trademark is a unique symbol, name, logo, word, or design used to identify goods or services of one company and distinguish them from others.

Trademark helps customers recognize brand identity.

### Examples

• Nike ✔️ (✔️ logo)
• Apple 🍎
• Tata
• Samsung

## Trademark can include:

• Words
• Symbols
• Logos
• Taglines
• Colors
• Sounds

# 2️⃣ Trademark Law in India

Trademark is governed by
Trade Marks Act, 1999

This law protects brand identity and prevents misuse.

# 3️⃣ Objectives of 

## 🎯 Main Objectives

• ✔️ Identify product origin
• ✔️ Distinguish goods/services
• ✔️ Protect brand reputation
• ✔️ Prevent confusion
• ✔️ Protect goodwill
• ✔️ Promote fair competition

### Example

Two companies selling shoes 👟
Trademark helps customer identify original brand.

# 4️⃣ Rights of Trademark Owner

Trademark owner gets exclusive rights:

### 🔐 Trademark Rights

• ✔️ Exclusive use of trademark
• ✔️ Right to sell products
• ✔️ Right to license trademark
• ✔️ Right to assign trademark
• ✔️ Right to sue for infringement
• ✔️ Right to protect goodwill

# 5️⃣ Protection of Goodwill

Goodwill means reputation of business.

Trademark helps protect:

• Brand name
• Customer trust
• Business reputation
• Market value

### Example

If someone copies brand logo, it damages goodwill.

# 6️⃣ Trademark Infringement

Trademark infringement means unauthorized use of registered trademark.
ve word like "Fresh Milk" is allowed.


### Examples

• Copying brand logo
• Using similar brand name
• Using confusingly similar mark
• Selling fake products

### Example:

"Adibas" copying "Adidas"

This creates confusion among customers.

# 7️⃣ Passing Off

Passing off is misrepresenting goods as another company's goods.

Even if trademark is not registered, passing off applies.

Passing Off Elements

• ✔️ Goodwill
• ✔️ Misrepresentation
• ✔️ Damage

### Example

Fake Apple logo on phone 🍎

Customer believes it's original.

# 8️⃣ Difference Between Infringement & Passing Off

Trademark Infringement	Passing Off
Registered trademark required	No registration required
Legal right based	Based on goodwill
Easier to prove	Harder to prove


# 9️⃣ Defences for Trademark Infringement

## 🛡️ Defences

• ✔️ Fair use
• ✔️ Honest use
• ✔️ Descriptive use
• ✔️ Prior use
• ✔️ Non-commercial use




# 1️⃣ Designs – Meaning

Design refers to shape, pattern, or appearance of product.

Design protects visual look of product.

### Examples:

• Mobile shape 📱
• Bottle design 🧴
• Car body 🚗
• Packaging design 📦

# 2️⃣ Design Law in India

Designs are protected under:

Designs Act, 2000

# 3️⃣ Objectives of Design Protection

## 🎯 Objectives

• ✔️ Protect product appearance
• ✔️ Encourage innovation
• ✔️ Prevent copying
• ✔️ Protect business value
• ✔️ Promote creativity

# 4️⃣ Rights of Design Owner

Design owner gets:

## 🔐 Rights

• ✔️ Exclusive use of design
• ✔️ Right to sell product
• ✔️ Right to license design
• ✔️ Right to assign design
• ✔️ Right to sue for infringement

# 5️⃣ Assignment of Designs

Design ownership can be transferred.

### Types

• Assignment by agreement
• Assignment by license
• Assignment by sale

### Example

Company sells mobile design to another company.

# 6️⃣ Design Infringement

Design infringement occurs when:

• Copying design
• Using similar design
• Selling copied design
• Importing copied design

### Example

Copying mobile shape.

# 7️⃣ Defences of Design Infringement

## 🛡️ Defences

• ✔️ Design not new
• ✔️ Design already published
• ✔️ Independent creation
• ✔️ No similarity
• ✔️ Functional design

### Example

If design already exists, no infringement.

# 8️⃣ Remedies for Design Infringement

## ⚖️ Legal Remedies

• ✔️ Injunction
• ✔️ Damages
• ✔️ Seizure
• ✔️ Profit recovery
