# 1️⃣ Define Trademark

### Answer:

A Trademark is a symbol, word, logo, or design used to identify goods or services of one company and distinguish them from others.

Trademark is protected under
Trade Marks Act, 1999

### Example

• Nike ✔️
• Apple 🍎
• Tata

# 2️⃣ What is Goodwill?

### Answer:

Goodwill is the reputation and customer trust of a business.

It helps attract customers and increase business value.

### Example

Customers trust branded products due to goodwill.

# 3️⃣ What is Passing Off?

### Answer:

Passing off means selling goods by misrepresenting them as another company's goods.

### Elements of Passing Off

• ✔️ Goodwill
• ✔️ Misrepresentation
• ✔️ Damage

### Example

Fake Apple logo on mobile 🍎

# 4️⃣ Define Design

### Answer:

Design refers to shape, pattern, or appearance of a product.

Design protection is provided under
Designs Act, 2000

### Example

• Mobile design 📱
• Bottle design 🧴

# 5️⃣ What is Trademark Infringement?

### Answer:

Trademark infringement means unauthorized use of registered trademark.

### Example

Using similar brand name like "Adibas" instead of "Adidas".

# 6️⃣ What is Design Assignment?

### Answer:

Design assignment means transfer of ownership of design to another person.

### Example

Company sells product design to another company.

# 7️⃣ What are Trademark Rights?

### Answer:

Trademark owner has following rights:

• ✔️ Exclusive use
• ✔️ Sell products
• ✔️ License trademark
• ✔️ Assign trademark
• ✔️ Sue for infringement

# 8️⃣ Define Design Infringement

### Answer:

Design infringement means copying registered design without permission.

### Example

Copying mobile shape 📱

1️⃣ Explain Objectives and Rights of Trademark

#### Objectives of Trademark

• ✔️ Identify goods
• ✔️ Protect brand reputation
• ✔️ Prevent confusion
• ✔️ Protect goodwill
• ✔️ Promote fair competition

#### Rights of Trademark Owner

• ✔️ Exclusive use
• ✔️ Right to sell
• ✔️ Right to license
• ✔️ Right to assign
• ✔️ Right to sue

# 2️⃣ Explain Protection of Goodwill in Trademark

Goodwill means business reputation.

Trademark protects goodwill by:

• Preventing copying
• Maintaining brand image
• Avoiding confusion
• Protecting customer trust

### Example

Fake brand damages original company goodwill.

# 3️⃣ Explain Passing Off with Example

Passing off is misrepresentation of goods.

### Elements

• Goodwill
• Misrepresentation
• Damage

### Example

Fake Nike shoes sold as original 👟

Customers get confused.

# 4️⃣ Explain Design Objectives and Rights
Objectives of Design

• ✔️ Protect appearance
• ✔️ Encourage innovation
• ✔️ Prevent copying
• ✔️ Promote creativity

#### Rights of Design Owner

• ✔️ Exclusive use
• ✔️ Sell product

• ✔️ Assign design

# 5️⃣ Explain Design Infringement and Defences

Design Infringement

### Occurs when:

• Copying design
• Using similar design
• Selling copied product

### Example

Copying mobile design.

### Defences of Design Infringement

• ✔️ Design not new
• ✔️ Already published
• ✔️ No similarity
• ✔️ Independent creation
• ✔️ Functional design
