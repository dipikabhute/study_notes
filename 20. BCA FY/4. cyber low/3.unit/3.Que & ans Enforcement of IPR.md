# Q1. Explain Civil Remedies in Intellectual Property Rights

## Answer:

Civil Remedies are legal actions taken by the Intellectual Property owner in civil court to stop infringement and claim compensation.

### Objectives of Civil Remedies

✔ Stop infringement
✔ Protect rights of owner
✔ Recover damages
✔ Prevent future misuse

### Types of Civil Remedies

#### 1. Injunction

An injunction is a court order to stop infringement.

### Types of Injunction

✔ Temporary Injunction
✔ Permanent Injunction
✔ Anton Piller Order
✔ Mareva Injunction

#### 2. Damages

✔ Compensation given to owner
✔ Covers financial loss

#### 3. Account of Profits

✔ Infringer pays illegal profits to owner

#### 4. Delivery up / Destruction

✔ Court orders destruction of fake goods

# Q2. Explain Criminal Remedies in Intellectual Property Rights

## Answer:

Criminal Remedies punish the infringer through fine or imprisonment.

### Objectives

✔ Punish infringer
✔ Prevent illegal activity
✔ Protect public interest

### Types of Criminal Remedies

#### 1. Imprisonment

✔ Jail punishment
✔ Usually 6 months to 3 years

#### 2. Fine

✔ Monetary penalty

#### 3. Seizure of Goods

✔ Police confiscates fake goods

#### 4. Search and Seizure

✔ Police searches premises

### Example

✔ Pirated software
✔ Fake branded goods
✔ Duplicate CDs

# Q3. Explain Border Security Measures

## Answer:

Border Security Measures protect Intellectual Property Rights at import and export level.

### Objectives

✔ Prevent fake products
✔ Protect brand reputation
✔ Stop illegal imports

### Border Security Measures

#### 1. Detention of Goods

✔ Customs stops suspicious goods

#### 2. Inspection of Goods

✔ Goods checked by customs

#### 3. Seizure of Goods

✔ Fake goods confiscated

#### 4. Destruction of Goods

✔ Illegal goods destroyed

# Q4. Explain Practical Aspects of Licensing

## Answer:

Licensing is an agreement where IP owner gives permission to another party to use intellectual property.

### Benefits of Licensing
For Licensor

✔ Earn royalty
✔ Expand market
✔ Reduce risk

### For Licensee

✔ Use brand name
✔ Reduce development cost
✔ Gain technology

# Q5. Explain Determinative Factors of Licensing

## Answer:

Determinative factors decide licensing agreement terms.

### Factors

✔ Market demand
✔ Nature of IP
✔ Territory
✔ Duration
✔ Royalty rate
✔ Competition

# Short Question & Answers

## Q1. What is Enforcement of IPR?

### Answer:

Enforcement of IPR means protecting intellectual property from unauthorized use through legal action.

## Q2. What is Civil Remedy?

### Answer:

Civil remedy is legal action taken in civil court to stop infringement and recover damages.

## Q3. What is Criminal Remedy?

### Answer:

Criminal remedy punishes infringer with fine or imprisonment.

## Q4. What is Licensing?

### Answer:

Licensing is permission given by IP owner to another party to use intellectual property.

### Answer:

Royalty is payment made by licensee to licensor for using intellectual property.

## Q6. What is Injunction?

### Answer:

Injunction is court order to stop infringement.

## Q7. What is Exclusive License?

### Answer:

Exclusive license allows only one licensee to use intellectual property.

## Q8. What is Non-Exclusive License?

### Answer:

Non-exclusive license allows multiple licensees.
