# Q1. What is Cyber Law?

## Answer:

Cyber Law refers to the legal framework that governs the use of computers, internet, and digital communication. It deals with cyber crimes, electronic commerce, digital signatures, and data protection.

# Q2. What is Internet Technology?

## Answer:

Internet technology refers to the system and tools used to connect computers worldwide. It includes protocols like TCP/IP, servers, clients, and communication networks.

# Q3. What is Cyber Jurisprudence?

## Answer:

Cyber Jurisprudence refers to legal principles that govern cyberspace and online activities. It deals with jurisdiction, liability, privacy, and security issues.

# Q4. What is Digital Contract?

## Answer:

A Digital Contract is an agreement formed electronically using the internet or digital communication.

## Example:

Online shopping agreement, software license agreement.

# Q5. What is Digital Signature?

## Answer:

Digital Signature is an electronic method used to verify the identity of a person and authenticate electronic documents.

# Q6. What is Cryptography?

## Answer:

Cryptography is the technique used to protect information by converting it into coded form to prevent unauthorized access.

# Q7. What is Certifying Authority?

## Answer:

Certifying Authority is an organization that issues digital signature certificates and verifies identity of users.

# Q8. What is Click Wrap Agreement?

## Answer:

Click Wrap Agreement is a digital contract where users accept terms and conditions by clicking "I Agree".

# Q11. Explain Cyber Jurisprudence

## Answer:

Cyber Jurisprudence refers to legal issues in cyberspace.

### Elements

#### 1. Jurisdiction

Determines which court has authority.

#### 2. Liability

Determines responsibility for cyber crime.

#### 3. Privacy

Protection of personal data.

#### 4. Security

Protection from cyber attacks.

# Q14. Explain Science of Cryptography

## Answer:

Cryptography protects information using encryption.

## Types of Cryptography

1. Symmetric Key Cryptography

Same key for encryption and decryption.

2. Asymmetric Key Cryptography

Two keys used:

✔ Public key
✔ Private key

## Advantages

✔ Secure communication
✔ Protect data
✔ Prevent hacking
