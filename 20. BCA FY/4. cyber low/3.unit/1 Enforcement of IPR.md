# 1. Enforcement of Intellectual Property Rights

## Meaning:

Enforcement of Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) refers to legal actions taken to protect intellectual property from infringement, misuse, or unauthorized use.

### Types of Enforcement:

➤ Civil Remedies
➤ Criminal Remedies
➤ Border Security Measures

# 2. Civil Remedies

## Meaning:

Civil remedies are legal actions taken in civil court to stop infringement and recover damages.

### Objectives of Civil Remedies

✔ Stop infringement
✔ Protect owner's rights
✔ Recover damages
✔ Prevent future misuse

### Types of Civil Remedies


# 1. Injunction

## Meaning:

A court order to stop infringement.

### Types of Injunction:

➤ Temporary Injunction
➤ Permanent Injunction
➤ Anton Piller Order
➤ Mareva Injunction

### 1. Temporary Injunction

✔ Granted before final judgment
✔ Prevents immediate damage

### 2. Permanent Injunction

✔ Given after final judgment
✔ Stops infringement permanently

### 3. Anton Piller Order

✔ Court allows search of premises
✔ Evidence can be seized
✔ Prevents destruction of proof

### 4. Mareva Injunction

✔ Freezes infringer's assets
✔ Prevents transfer of money or property

# 2. Damages

## Meaning:

Money compensation for loss caused by infringement.

### Types of Damages

➤ Actual damages
➤ Punitive damages
➤ Nominal damages

## 3. Account of Profits

✔ Court orders infringer to pay profits earned illegally

## 4. Delivery up / Destruction

✔ Court orders destruction of infringing goods
✔ Prevents further misuse

# 3. Criminal Remedies

## Meaning:

Criminal remedies punish the infringer with fine or imprisonment.

### Objectives

✔ Punish infringer
✔ Prevent illegal activities
✔ Protect public interest

## Types of Criminal Remedies

### 1. Imprisonment

✔ Infringer may face jail
✔ Usually 6 months to 3 years

### 2. Fine

✔ Monetary penalty
✔ Can be heavy depending on offence

### 3. Seizure of Goods

✔ Police confiscates infringing goods
✔ Stops distribution

### 4. Search and Seizure

✔ Police search premises
✔ Collect illegal copies

### Example

✔ Pirated Software
✔ Fake branded products
✔ Illegal copies of movies

# 4. Border Security Measures

## Meaning:

Protection of Intellectual Property at import/export level.

### Objectives

✔ Stop fake products
✔ Protect brand reputation
✔ Prevent illegal imports

### Border Enforcement Authorities

✔ Customs Department
✔ Government Authorities
✔ IP Right Holder

### Border Security Measures

#### 1. Detention of Goods

✔ Customs stops suspected goods

#### 2. Inspection of Goods

✔ Customs checks goods

#### 3. Seizure of Goods

✔ Fake products seized

#### 4. Destruction of Goods

✔ Illegal goods destroyed

### Example

✔ Fake Nike shoes
✔ Duplicate electronics
✔ Pirated CDs

# 5. Practical Aspects of Licensing
Licensing

## Meaning:

Licensing is a legal agreement where IP owner gives permission to another party to use intellectual property.

## 6. Benefits of Licensing

For Licensor

✔ Earn royalty income
✔ Expand market
✔ Reduce business risk
✔ Increase brand value

For Licensee

✔ Use established brand
✔ Reduce development cost
✔ Access technology
✔ Market advantage

# 7. Determinative Factors of Licensing

These factors decide licensing terms:

✔ Market demand
✔ Nature of IP
✔ Duration of license
✔ Territory
✔ Royalty rate
✔ Competition
✔ Legal regulations

# 8. Important Clauses in Licensing Agreement

### 1. Grant Clause

✔ Defines rights granted

### 2. Territory Clause

✔ Area where license is valid

### 3. Duration Clause

✔ Time period of license

### 4. Royalty Clause

✔ Payment terms

### 5. Confidentiality Clause

✔ Protects secret information

### 6. Termination Clause

✔ Conditions to end agreement

### 7. Quality Control Clause

✔ Maintain product quality

### 8. Sub-Licensing Clause

✔ Whether further licensing allowed

### 9. Licensing Clauses

### Exclusive License

✔ Only one licensee
✔ No one else allowed

### Non-Exclusive License

✔ Multiple licensees allowed

Sole License

✔ One licensee + owner also allowed

## Example of Licensing

✔ Microsoft licensing software
✔ Franchise licensing (McDonald's)
✔ Technology licensing
