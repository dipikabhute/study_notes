## Q1. Explain Objectives of Information Technology Act 2000

### Answer:

The Information Technology Act, 2000 was introduced to provide legal recognition to electronic transactions and to control cyber crimes.

### Objectives of IT Act 2000

✔ Provide legal recognition to electronic records
✔ Facilitate electronic commerce (E-commerce)
✔ Promote e-governance
✔ Prevent cyber crimes
✔ Provide legal recognition to digital signatures
✔ Protect privacy and data
✔ Establish Certifying Authorities

## Q2. Explain Section 43 – Penalty for Damage to Computer

### Answer:

Section 43 deals with unauthorized access or damage to computer systems.

### A person is liable under Section 43 if he:

✔ Accesses computer without permission
✔ Downloads data illegally
✔ Introduces virus
✔ Damages computer system
✔ Disrupts network
✔ Denies access to authorized user

### Punishment

✔ Compensation for damage caused
✔ Fine depending on loss

### Example

✔ Hacking computer
✔ Deleting files
✔ Installing virus

## Q3. Explain Section 66 – Hacking of Computer Systems

### Answer:

Section 66 deals with hacking with criminal intention.

### A person commits hacking if he:

✔ Destroys data
✔ Alters information
✔ Steals data
✔ Causes damage

With dishonest or fraudulent intention

### Punishment

✔ Imprisonment up to 3 years
✔ Fine up to ₹5 lakh
✔ Or both

### Example

✔ Bank hacking
✔ Password cracking
✔ Data theft

## Q4. Explain Section 67 – Publishing Obscene Information

### Answer:

Section 67 deals with publishing obscene content online.

### A person is guilty if he:

✔ Publishes obscene content
✔ Shares pornographic material
✔ Sends obscene messages

### Punishment
First Offence

✔ Imprisonment up to 3 years
✔ Fine up to ₹5 lakh

Second Offence

✔ Imprisonment up to 5 years
✔ Fine up to ₹10 lakh

### Example

✔ Posting obscene photos
✔ Sharing illegal videos

## Q5. Explain Section 72 – Breach of Privacy

### Answer:

Section 72 deals with breach of confidentiality and privacy.

### If a person:

✔ Accesses information legally
✔ Discloses without permission

### Then Section 72 applies.

Punishment

✔ Imprisonment up to 2 years
✔ Fine up to ₹1 lakh
✔ Or both

### Example

✔ Employee leaking company data
✔ Sharing private emails

## Q6. Explain Section 73 – Publishing False Digital Signature Certificate

### Answer:

Section 73 deals with publishing false digital signature certificates.

A person is guilty if he:

✔ Publishes false certificate
✔ Issues fake certificate
✔ Misleads users

### Punishment

✔ Imprisonment up to 2 years
✔ Fine up to ₹1 lakh
✔ Or both
