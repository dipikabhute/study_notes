# Information Technology Act, 2000

## Introduction

The Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act 2000) is the primary law in India dealing with cybercrime and electronic commerce.

It was enacted by the Government of India to provide legal recognition to electronic transactions and to control cyber crimes.

### Objectives of IT Act 2000

✔ Provide legal recognition to electronic records
✔ Facilitate e-commerce and e-governance
✔ Prevent cyber crimes
✔ Promote digital signatures
✔ Protect data and privacy
✔ Provide legal framework for digital communication

### Important Features of IT Act 2000

✔ Legal recognition to electronic records
✔ Legal recognition to digital signatures
✔ Establishment of Certifying Authorities
✔ Cyber crime penalties
✔ Protection of privacy

# Important Sections of IT Act 2000

## Section 43 – Penalty for Damage to Computer

### Meaning:

Section 43 deals with unauthorized access or damage to computer systems.

### If a person without permission:

✔ Accesses computer system
✔ Downloads data
✔ Introduces virus
✔ Damages computer
✔ Disrupts system
✔ Denies access to authorized user

Then the person is liable under Section 43

### Punishment

✔ Compensation for damage
✔ Fine up to ₹1 Crore (depending on damage)

### Example

✔ Hacking someone's computer
✔ Installing virus
✔ Deleting important files

## Section 66 – Hacking of Computer Systems

### Meaning

Section 66 deals with hacking with criminal intention.
### If a person:

✔ Destroys data
✔ Alters data
✔ Steals information
✔ Causes damage

## With dishonest or fraudulent intention, then Section 66 applies.

### Punishment

✔ Imprisonment up to 3 years
✔ Fine up to ₹5 lakh
✔ Or both

### Example

✔ Bank account hacking
✔ Password cracking
✔ Data theft

## Section 67 – Publishing Obscene Information

### Meaning

Section 67 deals with publishing obscene content online.

### If a person:

✔ Publishes obscene content
✔ Shares pornographic material
✔ Transmits obscene messages

Then Section 67 applies.

### Punishment

First Offence

✔ Imprisonment up to 3 years
✔ Fine up to ₹5 lakh

Second Offence

✔ Imprisonment up to 5 years
✔ Fine up to ₹10 lakh

### Example

✔ Posting obscene images
✔ Sharing illegal videos

## Section 72 – Penalty for Breach of Privacy

### Meaning

Section 72 deals with breach of confidentiality and privacy.

If a person:

✔ Accesses information legally
✔ But discloses it without permission

### Then Section 72 applies.

Punishment

✔ Imprisonment up to 2 years
✔ Fine up to ₹1 lakh
✔ Or both

### Example

✔ Employee leaking company data
✔ Sharing personal information

## Section 73 – Publishing False Digital Signature Certificate

### Meaning:

Section 73 deals with publishing false digital signature certificate.

### If a person:

✔ Publishes false certificate
✔ Issues fake digital certificate
✔ Misleads users

## Then Section 73 applies.

Punishment

✔ Imprisonment up to 2 years
✔ Fine up to ₹1 lakh
✔ Or both
